Facts! Oh My!


History

  1. The first residents of Myrtle Beach were the Waccamaw and Winyah tribes.

  2. In the 1800s and early 1900s, Myrtle Beach was known as "New Town." Myrtle Beach got its name from a contest to decide an official name. Mrs. F.E. Burroughs won this and came up with name due to the wax myrtle trees lining its coast.

  3. Seaside Inn (1901) was Myrtle Beach's first hotel.

  4. Myrtle Beach's Train Depot was built in 1936.

Pop Culture

  1. The country band Alabama was the house band for a place called "The Bowery" in Myrtle Beach.

  2. The movie Wild Hearts Can't Be Broken and the TV show Dawson's Creek were filmed in Myrtle Beach.

  3. Christopher Jones and Melissa Wyler are from Myrtle Beach.

  4. Sport's Illustrated was started in Myrtle Beach.

Geography

  1. There are 295.79 miles of street in Myrtle Beach.

  2. The city has 650 acres of public parks and recreation property.

  3. The beach has 60 miles of coastline.

  4. There used to be huge sand dunes where Kings Highway passes.

500 years of History...

The History of Machu Picchu can be split into 3 parts:

  1. Time as a royal estate (1438-1518)
  2. European expeditions after rediscovery (1867-1964)
  3. Modern preservation (1981-Present)

Machu Picchu under the Incan Empire

Scholars, such as Professor of Anthropology at Yale University Richard L. Burger, believe that construction of Machu Picchu began under the rule of Inca leader Pachacutec (c. 1420AD), as a royal estate. Around 750 people lived there at a time, mostly support staff known as yanaconas. Agriculture in Machu Picchu was done on hundreds of man-made terraces, considered to be works of engineering genius! These terraces were built to ensure drainage and soil fertility, all while protecting the mountain from erosion and landslides. Although Machu Picchu was a royal estate, it was not generationally passed down. Rather, the site was abandoned after about 80 years of use with the conquest of the Spanish.

Machu Picchu Today

Each and every stone in Machu Picchu was precisely cut to fit together so tightly that no mortar was needed to keep the walls standing.

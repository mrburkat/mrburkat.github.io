Fun Facts

  • Bogotá has been the capital of Colombia since 1819 and is also the country's largest city.
  • The city was originally called Bacatá ("The Lady of the Andes") when it was settled by the Muisca people.
  • Bogotá is located 2640 meters above sea level.
  • There are rainy and dry seasons, but the temperatures remain mostly consistent throughout the year.
  • The city is located 1000 kilometers from the equator.

Navigation

  • In Bogotá, carreras run south to north and calles run east to west.
  • Diagonal streets are referred to as transversales and diagonales.
  • Each street has a number, not a name, so it they can be located without a GPS.
